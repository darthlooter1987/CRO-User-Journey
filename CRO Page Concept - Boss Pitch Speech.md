# CRO Page Concept — Pitch Speech

*~3 minutes · Conversational delivery · Covers all 8 screens*

---

Hey — I want to walk you through a concept we've been working on for CRO. The pitch in one sentence: **give us your URL, and we'll give you a complete set of landing pages — one for each stage of the buying journey.** Awareness, Differentiation, Conversion, Loyalty — all four, ready to go.

The user we're designing for is a small business owner on Shopify. They have a product. They know they want more sales. But they don't understand funnels. They don't know why you'd send a social media ad to a different landing page than a retargeting ad. We're solving that.

Let me walk you through the experience — eight screens, end to end.

---

**Screen one is the entry point.** One input field. "Enter your product URL." That's all we ask for. No signup, no onboarding questionnaire. We did this on purpose — we want zero friction at the front door. Paste your link, hit "Generate My Pages," and you're in.

**Screen two is the brand extraction.** While they wait, we're scraping their site — pulling the logo, brand colors, fonts, product details, even analyzing their tone of voice. We show a step-by-step animation so they can see it happening in real time. The reason we show each step is to build confidence that something meaningful is happening behind the scenes — it's not a black box.

**Screen three is the brand review.** We present everything we found — color swatches, their logo, typography, detected products, and a summary of their brand voice. They can adjust anything or just approve it. The reason this screen exists is trust. We're about to generate four pages using *their* brand, so they need to believe we got it right before we go further.

**Screen four is where it gets interesting.** This is the funnel education screen. While we generate their pages in the background, we walk them through what a purchase funnel actually is. Each stage — Awareness, Differentiation, Conversion, Loyalty — fades in one by one with a plain-English explanation. We tell them what each page does, what kind of campaign it pairs with, and what mindset their visitor is in at that stage. The reason we do this *during* generation is that we're turning dead wait time into the most important onboarding moment in the product. By the time the pages are done, the user actually understands *why* they have four pages — not just that they do.

**Screen five is the dashboard.** Four funnel cards laid out left to right — Awareness, Differentiation, Conversion, Loyalty — with readiness scores and tooltips that reinforce what each stage is for. Below that, each page has a checklist. Some items are already done — hero sections, CTAs, comparison tables. Some are empty and need the owner's input — things like a real customer testimonial, their brand story, product photos. The reason for the checklists is that we can auto-generate maybe 70–80% of a good page, but the last 20% needs *their* knowledge. The checklist makes that obvious and actionable instead of overwhelming.

**Screen six is the page editor.** When they click into any page, they see a full preview of the generated landing page — hero section, content blocks, CTAs, everything — styled with their actual brand. On the right, there's a guidance sidebar with contextual hints for every section. Things like "this visitor has never heard of you — keep the tone welcoming" on the Awareness page, or "show the original price crossed out — it's proven to boost conversions" on the Conversion page. There's also a desktop/mobile toggle so they can preview both.

On the left, there's an edit panel with five categories — Content, Design, Page Settings, Advanced, and AI Tools. Everything from text editing to SEO tags to custom scripts to an AI copywriter. Most of these are behind a lock icon — and that's intentional. They're gated to the paid plan, which brings us to...

**Screen seven — the scaffold walkthrough.** When they first land on the dashboard, we run a three-step guided tour. It highlights the Awareness card, shows them the checklist items that need their input, and then tells them to click into any card to start editing. The reason we built this is that even with all the education, a dashboard full of content can still feel like a lot. The walkthrough narrows their focus to one thing: "start here."

**Screen eight is the paywall.** When they hit Publish or try to use a locked feature, they land on a subscription page. Two plans — Monthly at $25, Yearly at $20 with a "Best Value" badge. The yearly plan includes extras like A/B testing, analytics, custom domains, and early access. There's a 14-day free trial, no credit card required. The reason we gate at publish — not at entry — is that by this point they've seen their brand extracted, they've been educated on funnels, they've reviewed four fully generated pages, and they've clicked around the editor. They already feel the value. The paywall isn't a wall — it's a conversion point where the product has already sold itself.

---

So to zoom out — what we're really doing is solving four problems at once. First, we eliminate the cold-start problem. No blank canvas. Second, we teach them marketing fundamentals *inside the product*, not in a help doc they'll never read. Third, we give them a complete, branded campaign toolkit that's actually usable. And fourth, we monetize at the exact moment the user feels the most value — after they've seen what we built for them, not before.

The target user doesn't have a marketing team. They don't have an agency. They have a Shopify store and a budget for ads. This tool meets them where they are.

That's the concept. Happy to pull up the prototype and click through it live.
