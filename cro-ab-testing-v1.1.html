Why A/B Testing for Awareness?
Goal: Introduce your brand to a cold audience

The Cognitive Hurdle

In Awareness, you aren't optimizing a sale — you're optimizing for Attention. Cold traffic has a high bounce rate. If you test 8 combinations (MVT), you spread your precious "surviving" traffic too thin to reach a conclusion.

You need to validate the Core Hook. Is your audience motivated by "Results" or "Price"? "Luxury" or "Sustainability"? A/B gives you a clear, fast answer.

What to Test

🔄 Radical narrative shifts ✨ "Organic & Clean" vs "Professional" 🎯 Core Hook messaging

Bayesian Specifics by Business Size

🌱 Small / New Business

Uses a Strong Industry Prior. If a new beauty brand gets 5 sales from 10 visitors, the Bayesian model (anchored by a 2% industry prior) prevents them from thinking they've "cracked the code" prematurely. It keeps them from scaling on a fluke.

🏢 Big / Established Business

Uses a Brand Historical Prior. If they know their usual awareness ads convert at 4%, the model uses that as the baseline to see if the new creative is a true breakthrough.

⚡
Why MVT for Differentiation?
Goal: Prove superiority over competitors to a "Warm" audience

The Synergy Factor

Differentiation is about the Evidence Mix. Users at this stage are comparing features, prices, and reviews. A "Comparison Table" might work for a big brand because of their established trust, but a small brand might need a "Founder's Video" + "Comparison Table" to work.

MVT finds which combination of proof builds the strongest case — not just which single element works best.

What to Test

📊 Proof Points (Reviews vs Lab Results) 📋 Comparison Methods (Tables vs Bullets) 🎥 Founder's Video + Social Proof combos

The "Shrinkage" Benefit

If you test 3 different Competitor Comparison tables and 2 different Testimonial sections, the Shrinkage Strategy realizes if "Comparison Tables" generally fail for this industry — allowing the engine to stop testing those variations across the board, saving massive traffic.

🧠 How Shrinkage Works Here

All combinations with the same element are treated as family. If every combo with "Comparison Table Style A" underperforms, the engine borrows that signal — no need to wait for 1,000 clicks on each combination individually.

Why A/B + Bandit for Conversion?
Goal: Close the sale — this is your "Money Page"

Revenue Protection

This is the most sensitive page. A standard experiment that sends 50% of traffic to a "Loser" for a month is a disaster for a small business.

Conversion is usually about Friction. Is the form too long? Is the price clear? Is the CTA in the right place? A/B testing gives you a clean signal on these high-impact changes.

What to Test

💳 Offer structures (Free Trial vs Discount) 📍 CTA placement 💰 Payment options (Buy Now, Pay Later)

The Bandit: Your Star Feature

The Thompson Sampling (Bandit) is what makes this special. As soon as Version B (e.g., "Buy Now, Pay Later") starts winning, the app shifts the majority of traffic there immediately.

💡 The Selling Point

The client makes more money during the test. You aren't just "testing" — you are "Auto-Optimizing." This is a massive differentiator for your app. Revenue is protected from day one.

💜
Why MVT + Fractional Factorial for Loyalty?
Goal: Upsell, cross-sell, and re-engage previous customers

Micro-Segmentation

Loyalty traffic is often smaller but highly valuable. You want to find the "Sweet Spot" for returning users who already know your brand.

Returning customers respond to Personalization. MVT allows you to test the "Welcome Back" message, a "Recommended for You" product, and a "Loyalty Discount" simultaneously.

What to Test

👋 Personalization tokens 🎁 Specialized bundles 💝 "Thank You" gifts

The Informative Prior (CRM-Powered)

Since these are existing customers, the app can pull data from the client's CRM to set the Prior. (e.g., "This user segment usually converts at 15%"). This makes the test incredibly fast and accurate.

🔬 Fractional Factorial Design

Instead of testing every combination (which would need huge traffic), fractional factorial tests a strategically chosen subset that still reveals all the important effects — perfect for the smaller but high-value loyalty audience.
