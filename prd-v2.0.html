What's New

What Changed in v2.0

Key Change The Campaign Funnel dashboard screen has been completely redesigned. The old small funnel cards with readiness bars have been replaced by four large showcase cards with rich visual previews of each generated landing page — creating an immediate "a-ha" moment for the merchant. The guided tooltip tour has been upgraded to a 4-step walkthrough that visits each card individually.

Specific changes in this version:

  • Showcase Cards: Four large, visually rich cards replace the old horizontal funnel strip. Each card contains a scaled-down preview of the actual generated landing page (hero section, content blocks, CTAs, trust signals) — so the merchant immediately sees that real, tailored pages were built for them.
  • Staggered Reveal Animation: When the dashboard loads, the four cards animate in one by one with a slide-up + fade effect, building anticipation. A confetti burst fires specifically on cards that have reached 10/10 readiness — celebrating completion and rewarding the merchant for finishing a page. This creates a targeted moment of delight tied to achievement rather than a generic animation.
  • Enhanced Guided Tour: The 3-step scaffold walkthrough has been expanded to a 4-step tour — one step per page. Each tooltip explains what's already great about the generated page and specifically what the merchant needs to add (with time estimates). The overlay uses a modern backdrop blur effect.
  • Readiness Scoring (per card): Each showcase card shows a prominent readiness score (e.g., "7 out of 10") in a floating badge with an inline progress bar, plus a clear summary of remaining items ("2 items to add — 3rd differentiator & customer quotes"). The score ring and progress bar color uses a dynamic red-to-green scale (0 = red, 5 = amber, 10 = green) so merchants can instantly gauge page completeness at a glance.
  • 2x2 Grid Layout: The four cards are arranged in a 2-column grid instead of the previous 4-across horizontal strip, giving each card much more visual real estate and making the page previews legible at a glance.
  • Tour Overlay Redesign: The tour uses a modern frosted-glass overlay with larger, more readable tooltips that include a highlighted callout box for the specific action items the merchant needs to complete.
  • Hover Tooltips: Each card now reveals a tooltip on hover explaining what that funnel stage page does — matching the educational approach from v1.0's funnel cards but in the new showcase layout.
  • Awareness Page at 10/10: The Awareness page is now fully complete with a customer testimonial (Sarah M.) and a brand story (Arctic Norway origin). Its card is visually differentiated with a green "ready to publish" border, a green readiness badge, and a "Publish Now" action button — signalling to the merchant that this page is live-ready.
  • Card Info Area Background: The text/info area below the preview now uses funnel-stage-specific colored backgrounds — blue tones for Awareness, orange tones for Differentiation, green tones for Conversion, and purple tones for Loyalty — matching the "Four moments" color scheme and providing clear visual separation from the preview area above.
  • Checklist Buttons Redesigned: "Add now" actions in the checklists are now high-contrast filled buttons (blue background, white text with shadow) instead of plain text links. "View" buttons use a subtle blue chip style. "Complete" buttons on cards renamed to "Finalize now".
  • Funnel Stage Labels on Entry Screen: The "Four moments" section on the landing/entry screen now uses proper funnel stage names (Awareness, Differentiation, Conversion, Loyalty) instead of generic "Page 1/2/3/4" labels. Each card also includes a "Best for:" list inside a colored box, educating the merchant on which campaign types each page is designed for (e.g., Awareness → Social Ads, Display Ads, Content Marketing).
  • Product Images Integrated: Real product images (A1.webp — product box, A2.webp — packaging comparison) are now placed across all four landing pages. The Awareness hero features the product box alongside the headline. The Differentiation page shows the old-vs-new packaging comparison. The Conversion page replaces the empty image placeholder with the product photo. The Loyalty page uses the packaging update in the "What's New" section.
Section 1

Overview & Problem Statement

Small business owners using Shopify are often running paid or organic marketing campaigns without a clear understanding of why different campaigns need different landing pages. They tend to send all traffic to the same page — typically the homepage or a product page — regardless of where the customer is in their purchase journey.

This results in low conversion rates, high bounce rates, and wasted ad spend, because a customer who has never heard of the brand requires a fundamentally different experience than one who is ready to buy.

The Core Insight Different stages of the customer journey require different emotional jobs to be done. A single landing page cannot optimally serve all of them. CRO (Conversion Rate Optimisation) means serving the right message at the right moment.

Revmatics solves this by generating fit-for-purpose landing pages tailored to the merchant's funnel stage — starting with just a URL input. The tool reads the merchant's site, understands their brand and product, and creates purpose-built pages for each stage of the customer journey.

Section 2

Primary User Story

Primary User Story

"As a small business owner with a Shopify account, I need to be educated on marketing activities in order for me to see the value in using the CRO tool. I need to understand what the stages of a purchase funnel are and why I am using different landing pages to facilitate different outcomes in different campaigns."

This user story reflects two intertwined needs:

  • Education: The merchant must first understand why different landing pages exist before they trust the tool enough to use it.
  • Execution: Once convinced, they need a frictionless path to actually generating those pages for their store.

The v1.0 mock-up focuses primarily on the educational onboarding experience and the URL-input-to-page-generation flow, establishing the product's core value proposition.

Section 3

The Purchase Funnel Model

The product is built around a four-stage purchase funnel model. Each stage represents a distinct moment in the customer's relationship with the brand, and each requires a landing page engineered for a different psychological and commercial outcome.

Awareness

Customer discovers the brand for the first time

Consideration

Customer evaluates and compares options

Conversion

Customer is ready to buy — remove friction

Loyalty

Post-purchase retention and advocacy

The tool generates one optimised landing page per funnel stage, each with distinct copy strategy, CTA structure, social proof placement, and visual hierarchy.

Section 5

Product Flow — v2.0

The v2.0 user journey follows a six-step flow (Steps 1-4 unchanged from v1.0, Step 5 redesigned):

  1. URL Input (Screen 1): The merchant enters their Shopify store URL on the landing page. The hero section educates them on the four page types before they input anything.
  2. Site Analysis (Screen 2): The tool crawls the merchant's site in real-time, extracting brand voice, product information, colour palette, and key selling points. A live progress indicator keeps the user engaged.
  3. Brand Summary (Screen 3): The extracted brand intelligence is surfaced to the merchant for review and light editing — ensuring the generated pages reflect their actual identity.
  4. Page Selection (Screen 4): The merchant selects which funnel stage(s) they want to build a landing page for, with plain-English explanations of each stage's purpose.
  5. Showcase Dashboard (Screen 5 — REDESIGNED in v2.0): The four generated pages are presented as large showcase cards in a 2x2 grid. Each card contains a miniaturised but visually rich preview of the actual page content, creating an immediate "wow" moment. Cards reveal with staggered animations, followed by an automated 4-step guided tour that walks the merchant through each page — explaining what's great and what they need to add. Clicking any card opens the full page editor.
  6. Export & Publish (Screen 6): The merchant can export the page to Shopify, download as HTML, or schedule a later activation.
Design Principle Every screen in the flow includes contextual education — the merchant should leave each step understanding not just what happened, but why it helps them convert better.

Section 7

UX & Design Principles

  • Education-first: Every screen teaches the merchant something about CRO and funnel strategy, not just task completion.
  • Progressive disclosure: Complexity is introduced only as needed. The URL input is the sole entry point — everything follows naturally from there.
  • Brand-reflective output: Generated pages should feel like they were hand-crafted for the specific merchant, not generic templates.
  • Trust through transparency: The site analysis step shows the merchant exactly what data was extracted and allows corrections — building confidence in the tool.
  • Mobile-first preview: Given that Shopify merchants' customers are predominantly mobile, the preview defaults to mobile view.
  • Jargon avoidance: All labels and tooltips use plain business language ("the page that turns browsers into buyers") rather than marketing-speak ("high-converting bottom-of-funnel asset").
